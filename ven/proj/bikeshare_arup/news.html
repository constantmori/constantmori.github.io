So, what exactly is this Bike Share Hackathon/Design Jam/Fun Day?

Bianca Wylie
July 23, 2014
We're taking the hackathon and giving it a good, healthy twist.
 Bike Share TO is releasing its open data sets for the first time ever - and we're inviting you to dig in first. (Don't worry - we've already cleaned it up). The Toronto Open Data portal has other data sets of note/interest to consider, including bicycle parking, bike trails and others. To get an idea of the data used and entries created, check out this event from the Bay Area Data Challenge: http://www.bayareabikeshare.com/datachallenge
 Not only do you get 3 days of hacking, but you also get access to world-class tech talks Friday night, mentors who actually hack and a good mix of serious devs and community experts judges.
 As far as format goes, we will be defining some of the current challenges in getting more people on bikes in Toronto and asking for your ideas on how to address them. We’ll be working with cyclists, City staff, transportation planning professionals and cycling activists before, during and after the event to make sure we keep our work on track.
You also get full access to the Community Adventure Fun Days on the other floor on Saturday. Come down and meet city open data team members, city planners and transportation planners, check out the design and policy jam and feel free to get your kids involved.
 Judging is also getting a good kick in the pants. Whether you want a hardcore hack weekend or to come up with a great idea, we've got a category for you. We won't judge your database based on how pretty the front-end is. Promise. We're breaking up judging into a variety of categories, from policy to data to hardware to grab bag. And we swear to everything we hold sacred that no one will win in more than 1 category.
 What can I win?
Lifelong friendships, glory and a freezie. No, seriously. Prizes TBD but expect swag, bike accessories and Bike Share memberships as well as the opportunity to get published on the city portal. 
 We'll be implementing a #hackgoeson model to continue apps after "final judging", so you can continue to access mentors and hackers to keep building this thing.
 We are not a hit-it-and-quit-it society.
We believe in at least medium-term relationships over one-night stands, and offer everyone the community to continue working if they want. We worked out of the super sexy People and Code offices from January-June and hackers were able to present prototypes at Go Open Data Day. We don't know what's next, but we're not going to leave you. Unless you want us to. In which case we will. 
 Why $30 to register?
The main two reasons for a cover fee are to ensure that we raise enough money to feed you throughout the weekend and to get the event in your calendar and keep it there!
So go on, register today:
http://www.bikesharearup-hackathonde.squarespace.com  
If you have any questions for us do leave them in the comments. See you soon!

Partnering with Bitmaker Labs -- our rad venue!!

Michael Markieta
July 7, 2014
We’re happy to announce that we’re partnering with Bitmaker Labs as our venue host for the Bike Share Toronto hackathon! 
Bitmaker Labs is Canada's leading web development learning centre. They aim to empower tech enthusiasts to build their ideas. What better place to host this hack? As one of key partners, Bitmaker will not only provide the space, but also offer great values alignment, as well as support with the Friday night lightning tech teaches.
Recently, Bianca and Naomi had a great meeting with Lidia Sienkowska, Community and Student Experience, at Bitmaker, to firm up all the details for the event. Lidia and her team at Bitmaker are really excited about it as many of them are avid cyclists, eager to make cycling in Toronto better.
After a really energizing chat with Lidia, we were reminded about why we are so excited about this hack:
  • It’s a chance for all participants to learn more about the Bike Share program and support its expansion;
  • There is a lot of overlap between the cycling and tech community in Toronto and so a lot of potential for powerful collaboration;
  • There is an opportunity for for planners, developers, urbanists, cyclists and others to participate on either a project or policy level (or both!);
  • There is an opportunity to create of educational opportunities to support ongoing tech skill development and to offer mentorship from the tech community to event participants;
  • There is an opportunity to connect the physical realm of cycling with the online and digital creations that can support it, and;
  • It will be a family-friendly community event to celebrate and support cycling in Toronto.

We're busy making this hack happen!

Michael Markieta
June 23, 2014
It's been an exciting two months since I first got the attention of Bike Share Toronto with a tweet that prompted a conversation about running a cycling-specific hackathon. Hi, my name is Michael Markieta and I am excited to write about the progress of what will surely be (and what we are calling) a fantastic hackathon and design jam.
One of my roles as an organizer will be to lead the development of this website, keeping it up to date with confirmed information about the event.
I would like to take this time to encourage all individuals with any interest in this event to register. Hacker? We have you covered. Artist? Let the data be your oyster. Cartographer? We ❤ maps too! None of the above? Tell us why you're interested – yes we do want to hear from you too!
